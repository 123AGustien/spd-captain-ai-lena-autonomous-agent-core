FIN-005 — Inflation Shock

Rule Information

Rule ID: FIN-005
Domain: Financial Resilience
Category: Inflation Risk
Status: Active
Version: 1.0
Repository: Sextant Rule Library

Purpose

Evaluate the resilience of the financial system during periods of elevated inflation and assess the potential cascading effects across interconnected economic and operational domains.

Scenario

A sustained increase in inflation driven by supply disruptions, rising energy prices, currency depreciation, or strong demand places pressure on households, businesses, financial institutions, and public finances.

The scenario evaluates:

- Inflation trends
- Consumer purchasing power
- Interest rate pressure
- Business operating costs
- Financial market confidence

Primary Indicators

- Consumer Price Index (CPI)
- Core Inflation Rate
- Producer Price Index (PPI)
- Interest Rate Changes
- Wage Growth
- Exchange Rate Movements
- Energy and Commodity Prices

Risk Levels

GREEN

Inflation remains within the target range.

Price stability is maintained and economic conditions remain balanced.

No contingency actions required.

YELLOW

Early signs of inflationary pressure.

Monitor:

- CPI trends
- Food and energy prices
- Interest rate expectations
- Currency movements

Action:

Increase monitoring frequency and review inflation-sensitive sectors.

ORANGE

Persistent inflation pressure.

Indicators:

- Inflation exceeds target range
- Rising borrowing costs
- Declining purchasing power
- Increasing business costs

Potential Cascade:

Inflation Shock
↓
Interest Rate Increases
↓
Reduced Consumer Spending
↓
Economic Slowdown

Action:

Strengthen monitoring, assess financial resilience, and prepare contingency measures.

RED

Severe inflation instability.

Indicators:

- Rapid inflation acceleration
- Significant monetary tightening
- Sharp decline in consumer confidence
- Widespread economic stress

Potential Cascade:

Inflation Crisis
↓
Financial Market Stress
↓
Banking Sector Pressure
↓
Cross-Domain Systemic Risk

Action:

Activate emergency economic resilience procedures and coordinate systemic response.

Cross-Domain Dependencies

Financial (FIN)
↓
Infrastructure (INF)
↓
Cyber (CYB)
↓
Data Centre Operations (DC)

Inflation-driven economic stress may propagate operational and financial risks across interconnected domains.

Contingency Actions

- Monitor inflation indicators continuously
- Review interest rate impacts
- Assess business and consumer resilience
- Evaluate systemic contagion risks
- Increase reporting frequency
- Escalate to institutional risk management when thresholds are exceeded
