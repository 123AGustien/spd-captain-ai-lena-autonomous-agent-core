# FIN-002 — Bond Outflow Stress

## Rule Information

Rule ID: FIN-002  
Domain: Financial Resilience  
Category: Sovereign Bond Market  
Status: Active  
Version: 1.0  
Repository: Sextant Rule Library  

## Purpose

Evaluate the resilience of the financial system during periods of significant foreign investor withdrawals from sovereign bond markets and assess the potential cascading effects across connected domains.

## Scenario

A sustained sell-off of government bonds by domestic or foreign investors reduces market liquidity and increases borrowing costs.

The scenario evaluates:

- Bond market liquidity
- Government bond yields
- Foreign investor participation
- Capital outflows
- Financial market confidence

## Primary Indicators

- Government bond yields
- Foreign investor bond holdings
- Daily bond trading volume
- Bond market volatility
- Capital outflow trends
- Credit spread movements

## Risk Levels

### GREEN

Normal bond market conditions.

Liquidity is stable and market confidence remains strong.

No contingency actions required.

### YELLOW

Early signs of reduced bond market liquidity.

Monitor:

- Bond yields
- Foreign investor activity
- Trading volumes

Action:

Increase monitoring frequency and review liquidity conditions.

### ORANGE

Significant bond outflows causing market stress.

Indicators:

- Rapid increase in bond yields
- Accelerating foreign investor withdrawals
- Reduced market liquidity
- Increased market volatility

Potential Cascade:

Bond Outflow  
↓  
Higher Borrowing Costs  
↓  
Liquidity Tightening  
↓  
Financial Market Stress

Action:

Prepare contingency measures and enhance market surveillance.

### RED

Severe bond market disruption.

Indicators:

- Extreme bond yield movements
- Major investor withdrawals
- Liquidity breakdown
- Broad financial instability

Potential Cascade:

Bond Market Crisis  
↓  
Funding Stress  
↓  
Banking Sector Pressure  
↓  
Systemic Financial Risk

Action:

Activate emergency response procedures and systemic financial stability measures.

## Contingency Actions

- Monitor government bond markets continuously
- Assess liquidity conditions
- Review funding requirements
- Evaluate systemic contagion risks
- Increase reporting frequency
- Escalate to institutional risk management when thresholds are exceeded
