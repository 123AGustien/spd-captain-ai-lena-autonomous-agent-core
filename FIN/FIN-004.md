# FIN-004 — Banking Stress

## Rule Information

- Rule ID: FIN-004
- Domain: Financial Resilience
- Category: Banking System Stability
- Status: Active
- Version: 1.0
- Repository: Sextant Rule Library

## Purpose

Evaluate the resilience of the banking sector during periods of financial stress and assess potential cascading effects across interconnected systems.

## Scenario

Banks experience increasing financial pressure caused by liquidity constraints, credit deterioration, market instability, or loss of confidence.

## Trigger Conditions

- Banking liquidity deterioration
- Increased non-performing loans
- Credit quality deterioration
- Deposit outflow pressure
- Loss of market confidence
- Counterparty risk escalation

## Risk Assessment

Assess:

1. Banking system liquidity
2. Credit quality
3. Capital resilience
4. Deposit stability
5. Counterparty exposure
6. Interbank dependencies
7. Systemic cascade potential

## Decision Logic

### LOW

Continue monitoring banking system indicators.

### MEDIUM

Activate preventive banking resilience measures.

### HIGH

Activate financial stabilization mode and protect critical banking functions.

## Recommended Actions

1. Monitor banking liquidity and stability indicators.
2. Assess exposure to stressed financial institutions.
3. Review counterparty and settlement dependencies.
4. Protect critical payment and settlement functions.
5. Evaluate deposit and funding stability.
6. Monitor systemic contagion pathways.
7. Coordinate stabilization measures where required.

## Golden Rule

OBSERVE → VERIFY → ASSESS → DECIDE → ACT → UPDATE

## Expected Outcome

Maintain banking system resilience and limit the propagation of banking stress into wider financial and operational systems.
